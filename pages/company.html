    • About Sensei Esports

    Founders Eric Bigg and Nick Smith have been developing esports talent for nearly a decade and since Senseis inception in February 2015 have seen dozens of players move on to represent their games at the highest of levels. The organisation has been built around three key principles: professionalism, integrity and infrastructure which has been key to the growth and development of our players, staff and streamers giving them the best possible opportunity to turn their gaming passion into careers.

    Sensei History

    Sensei Esports was founded in Feburary 2015, founded by former Pulse Esports owner Eric Biggs and co-founded by Nick Smith former EyesOnU Manager and Head Coach. Sensei Esports, whilst not necessarily under that name, has been around for almost three years now!

    In 2015, Eric Biggs, former owner of Pulse Esports, created a new venture again into the world of competitive online gaming. Pulse itself had a plethora of success during its time, almost reaching LCS twice with their League of Legends teams as well as having an extremely good Halo team. Eric went on to sell Pulse, and created Enigma Esports. He wasn't alone however, as joining him was Nick Smith. Nick was a talented League coach, and had famously coached a team of Gold-Platinum players up to the Challenger Series! Nick also worked with the organisation EyesOnU, before joining Eric.

    Together, Nick and Eric have decades of experience, both in business and in the ever-expanding Esports market. As time went on, Enigma was able to expand and bring on more members, and in the late stages of 2015, Enigma took the decision to re-brand to Sensei Esports, a name that is growing every day!
